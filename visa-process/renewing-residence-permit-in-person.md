## ğŸŒ Overview: Online vs. In-Person

You have two methods for renewing your visa. Here are the trade-offs:

- **Online Method (Online Guide):**
  - **Pros:** Only requires mailing documents, not visiting the office.
  - **Cons:** Requires a lot of technical setup (MyNumber Card, IC card reader, JPKI software, etc.).
  - [See the Complete Online Guide here](./renewing-residence-card-online.md)
- **In-Person Method (This Guide):**
  - **Pros:** Requires no special software or hardware. You just need your documents.
  - **Cons:** You must physically go to the immigration office **twice**. Wait times are famously long (often 2-5 hours _per visit_).

### 1. Download and Fill the Forms

You must download and print the official application forms.

  - **PDF (`.pdf`) format:** [https://www.moj.go.jp/isa/content/930004102.pdf](https://www.moj.go.jp/isa/content/930004102.pdf)

- **Fill and Sign:** It is best to fill the forms using the Excel version, then print them. You must **physically sign** the "For Applicant" form with a pen.

#### Tips for Filling Forms

- **Write Your Name EXACTLY as it Appears on Your Residence Card:** This is a critical check. Look at your current residence card. Your name must be written in the exact same order and case.
  - _Example:_ On the application, write `CHOWDHURY ISHMAM ABIR` (all uppercase, family name first) if that is what is printed on your card.
- **Get Organization Part Filled:** Give the "For Organization" forms to your company's HR or school's administration office _at least_ one or two weeks in advance.
- **Proofread:** Double-check all details.

## visit 1ï¸âƒ£ Step 2: First Visit â€“ Submitting Your Application

1. **Find Your Office:** You must go to the regional ISA office that has jurisdiction over your address.
2. **Go Early:** Arrive _before_ the office opens (e.g., by 8:30 AM). This is the most important tip to avoid waiting all day.
3. **Get a Number:** Go to the counter for "Residence Procedures" (`åœ¨ç•™é–¢ä¿‚`) and get a numbered waiting ticket.
4. **Wait:** Wait for your number to be called. This can take several hours.
5. **Submit Documents:** When called, go to the counter and hand over your entire application packet (forms, passport, and residence card).
6. **Get Your Card Stamped:** The officer will check your documents. If everything is in order, they will take your current residence card and stamp the back of it with `ç”³è«‹ä¸­` (shinsei-chuu, "Application in Progress").
7. **Receive Your Passport:** They will return your passport and your residence card (with the new stamp). This stamp legally extends your period of stay for up to two months past your expiration date, so you are safe.
8. **Go Home:** Your first visit is complete.

## âœ‰ï¸ Step 3: Waiting for Approval (The Postcard)

Now, you must wait. This can take anywhere from two weeks to two months.

- You will receive an official postcard (`ã¯ãŒã => hagaki`) in the mail from the immigration office.
- This postcard is your "Notice of Approval." It means your new residence card is ready to be collected.
- **Do not lose this postcard!** You must bring it with you to collect your card.

7. **Receive Your New Card:** They will give you your passport and your new residence card (they will punch a hole in your old one and return it to you). Your renewal is complete.

## ğŸ’¡ Tips for Visiting the Immigration Office

- **Go Early, Go Early, Go Early:** Arrive at least 30 minutes before the office officially opens. You will be in a queue, but it's much better than arriving at 10 AM.
- **Bring a Book:** You will be waiting for hours. Bring a book, a portable battery for your phone, or work to do.
- **Bring Food/Water:** The cafeterias (if they exist) are not always great. Bring a snack.
- **Check the Date:** Avoid Mondays, Fridays, and the days immediately after a public holiday. These are the busiest times.
